# Goblin Empire — Economy Balance (faucet ↔ sink analysis)

Generated by the economy lane from the live database (`src/data/*.json`).
Purpose: check the player economy won't runaway-inflate as we head toward an MMO
with a Grand Exchange. Re-run the analysis after any drop-table / recipe / gp
changes.

## Coin faucets (where gold enters circulation)

| Source | Reading |
|---|---|
| Monster coin drops | **21 / 60** monsters drop coins. Avg **~20 coins/kill** (range 2.4–93). |
| Level scaling | **Not** correlated with combat level — several early mobs give coins; **all four bosses (cmb 72–82) drop 0 coins.** Bosses pay out in high-value *materials*, not gold. |
| Selling goods | The **primary** coin faucet: players sell gathered/crafted/looted items on the Grand Exchange (NPC liquidity) or to shops (44 shops buy from players). |

**Takeaway:** the coin supply is intentionally *material-driven* — you get rich by
producing and selling, not by killing for gold. Healthy for an MMO (resists the
classic "monsters mint infinite gold" inflation).

## Coin sinks (where gold leaves circulation)

- **GE 2% sell tax** — skims every player sale (implemented, tracked in `geTax`).
- **Shop purchases** — coins paid to NPC stores for tools/supplies.
- **Food consumption** — destroys the item (indirect: reduces sellable supply).
- **132 value-destroying recipes** — outputs worth less gp than inputs; act as
  material sinks.

## Material flow

- **690** item types are produced (node outputs + drops + recipe outputs).
- **97** distinct item types are consumed as recipe inputs.
- **601** items are *terminal* (produced, never a recipe input). By category:
  168 Equipment · 96 Tool · 89 Quest/Build · 84 Consumable · 49 Drop Material ·
  46 Resource · 43 Processed · **24 Unique/Cosmetic** · 2 Ammo.
- Terminal ≠ inflation for most of these: Equipment/Tools are **equipped**, food is
  **eaten**, quest items are **turned in** — all consumed by *play*, not by recipes.

## Crafting value

- **187** recipes add gp value (output ≥ inputs; total +38,947 gp of value created).
- **132** recipes destroy gp value (−29,419 gp; material sinks).
- Net roughly value-neutral → crafting isn't a runaway gold printer.

## Risks & recommendations

1. **New-player coin flow.** With a weak monster coin faucet, brand-new players
   rely on selling starter drops. Verify early mobs + shop sell-prices give enough
   starting liquidity, or add a small guaranteed coin drop to tutorial mobs.
2. **Cosmetic oversupply.** The **24 unique/cosmetic trophies** are terminal with
   **only the GE tax** as a coin-side sink and *no* item sink. If they drop too
   freely, their GE price → floor. Consider rarity tuning or a cosmetic sink
   (e.g. a collector NPC that consumes duplicates).
3. **Boss payouts.** Bosses give 0 coins by design; ensure their material drops
   have **deep, ongoing crafting demand** so they retain GE value long-term.
4. **When multiplayer lands,** the GE tax rate becomes the main macro dial for
   controlling the money supply — keep it adjustable server-side.
